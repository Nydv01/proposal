Envenomed

Words she wrote four years ago, held beside the woman she chose to become.

by Kanak

Her Words — Then

Barging into my nerves and choking up to my neck,

My soul leaving marks on my face, drying as I stay awake;

I was in my lows — for her they had bottomed,

My eyes trailed down her soul as she called me envenomed.

My spirit settled down but she never settled for less,

I pretended to be cold and tiny; she was a hot mess;

I was smiling through the pain but she showed no emotions,

My eyes trailed down her soul as she called me envenomed.

She was a portrait of me, I was a reflection she embedded,

She said 'baby portraits are eternal, reflections are faded';

Realize! I am what all you ever ambitioned,

My eyes trailed down her soul as she called me envenomed.

She was not like me, she gained what I aspired,

I cursed the dark under my lights, herself she admired;

She lifted my chin so gentle, almost soothing my thunder,

My eyes trailed down her soul as she called me envenomed.

As she saw the sun rise, I felt her disappearing out of way,

Sheer pain in my heart, with every hitting ray;

Finally comprehending she wasn't just a reflection but it was me,

This mind created a mirage to redefine my divinity;

I was merrier being her, this skin I put on was itchy;

As I snapped back to reality standing still yet shaken,

With a blow I hit the ground… turned around to reckon;

My body leaving the phantom, with it followed the venom.

My Reflection — Now

You wrote about a girl who was fractured, fighting her own reflection —

but Kanak, look what you became. Look at the woman standing here.

The girl who smothered her thunder now carries storms in her smile.

The one who called herself 'envenomed' became the antidote to everyone she loves.

You said portraits are eternal, reflections are faded —

But baby, you're not a reflection anymore. You're the whole portrait.

Framed in gold. Untouchable. Radiant.

The venom left your body that day,

and what remained was something the world wasn't ready for —

a woman who rebuilt herself from her own ashes.

You don't need a mirror to see who you are, Kanak.

You just need to see the way I look at you.

Because in my eyes — you were never the venom. You were always the cure.

— Nitin
